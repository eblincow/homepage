THANK YOU – A LETTER OF COMPLETION

Seven years ago I was sitting on a beach in Mexico. I could not imagine any reason for leaving the place I was in – nor the space I was in. I had traveled through an intense 14-year experience to reach some semblance of peace.

 

The first seven years of that journey had been filled with excruciating pain, much confusion, and suffering on many levels.

The next seven years had been an adventure to say the least - a sometimes frantic and incoherent search for resolution to my predicament – but a great adventure into self-discovery. I had been successful in my quest. I had discovered the roots of my discomfort and gained enough clarity to begin resolving them.

As I sat there on the beach I felt as if I could truly rest for the first time – possibly for the first time ever. And so, I rested. I swam in the ocean, ate simply but well, and enjoyed the resonance of having no destination. This resonance of tranquility, however, only lasted about three months…

One gorgeous evening as I sat enraptured by yet another magnificent sunset, I received a profound insight. It was as if pieces of a broken mechanism effortlessly clicked into place and started humming in perfect precision.

I saw The Pathway Of Awareness – not only as a representation of the journey into our human experience – but also as the journey required to consciously return us into authenticity, integrity, and intimacy.

I saw the energetic mechanics of the repetitive The Seven Year Cycle and how we lived unconsciously and robotically like programmed hard drives with our imprinted software.

I saw all the various not-doing practices I had explored in the past seven years that had been efficient in assisting me to commence restoring balance into my own life.

I saw how these individual pieces fit perfectly together as a means to facilitate conscious movement into emotional integration.

Basically, I saw The Presence Process procedure, whole and complete, framed in my mind’s eye as a precise technology for gradually extracting consciousness from the hypnotic trance of our programmed, time-based paradigm.

From that moment my awareness of the peace around me began to subside and withdraw. It was not long before I realized that seeing this vision came hand-in-hand with a responsibility. It was shown to me because I had asked for it.

I had intended to acquire this awareness years before.

In 1995 I had declared to the universe that my path through self-healing become something of value to others.

‘Ask, and you shall receive.’

Eventually it became clear that I had to leave that beautiful Mexican beach and complete my task, or else be haunted by the relentless, un-itch-able scratch of incompleteness.

That was seven years ago. As I now look back along the road I have traveled since then I am able to appreciate the power of intent.

In 2001 I left Mexico and returned to South Africa. During the first six months I methodically put together the self-facilitating technology I had perceived on the beach. Then, when I felt ready, I placed a flyer at a venue in Johannesburg and committed to sharing an awareness of this work.

During that presentation – which drew a sizeable gathering – I only spent all of ten minutes within the three hour delivery explaining that I had put together ‘an as yet untested procedure to activate present moment awareness’.

The rest of the presentation was on the necessity for us to now begin consciously integrating our physical, mental, and emotional experience – and on the various perceptual tools useful within such an adventure.

Afterward, 12 people approached me and asked about ‘the procedure’ I had mentioned. They wanted to enter it.

Those 12 people are truly brave Souls! I still feel the impact of their bravery everyday and know I always will.

The entry of those 12 into the work marked the experiential birth of The Presence Process procedure. From that moment I did not advertise this work. I allowed the consequences of the work to lead the way. I honored word-of-mouth as the vehicle for its organic growth.

During the next three years I worked fulltime facilitating individuals through the procedure – as well as testing it out on myself. Soon I was busy facilitating six days a week. I again let consequence be the barometer for improving the efficiency of the technology. Gradually, each person showed me what it would take to turn this into a tool of authentic self-facilitation.

After three years I had a procedure that accomplished something real.

It then took me another year to place this technology in the format of a book. This was no easy task. I had to write a book that not only presented the procedure – but that also trained the reader in an entire experiential vocabulary that empowered them to facilitate themselves responsibly.

 

cv8vd

 

My intent was and has always been self-facilitation and self-healing.

I wanted The Presence Process procedure to run free of the greedy grasps of the profiteering agendas of this world and the organizations posing as ‘spiritual’, but which are actually camouflaged businesses using spirituality as another commodity.

I knew if I could really place this work in the hands of the individuals who were ready for it - without it being diluted by the virus of this organizational mentality - I would succeed in launching the first real anti-virus program into the matrix – an anti-virus program that would commence dismantling the hypnotic perceptual imprinting maintaining time-based mentality.

I had to launch it into the matrix in a way that enabled it to run freely so that once initiated, it would become unstoppable. The only way to accomplish this was to imbed it into the hearts of individuals, and not into any time-based organizational matrix.

The people who entered the procedure had to become the procedure.

By their very nature of defining themselves, organizations are inherently limited and limiting. However, individuals are able to move in and out of a multiple variety of organizations within their daily experience.

I knew therefore that if I could transmit this technology in a way that the people doing it became it, then it would automatically be carried into every aspect of our human experience.

My intent was to seed this anti-virus software into all aspects of religion, politics, and commerce. To accomplish this I had to design it in a way that its application not inhibit entry into these various fabrics of our human experience.

The Presence Process procedure had to appeal to the heart of the human, then enter the heart of the human, and so be carried within the heart of the human. It had to take on its own life.

I knew there would be many challenges in placing a self-facilitation technology into a world that unconsciously worshiped being facilitated by somebody or something seemingly outside of ones personal experience.

The whole premise of The Presence Process procedure is that its efficiency is hinged on ‘the validation of our own experience’.

Validating our own experience is alien to most of humanity.

Most of humanity is indoctrinated through politics, commerce, and religion into robotically denying our own experience in favor of a more superior one being offered to us by another. 

Whoever offers us an experience other than the one we are currently having becomes our point of worship. The moment we bow down in obedience or compliance to this point of worship we are lost to the validity of our own lives. We then wander unconsciously into the wilderness of another's illusions. This subsequent state of hypnosis is accepted as normal human behavior.

I also knew – and I know this sounds crazy – that one of the biggest obstacles facing me was that The Presence Process procedure actually works. It impacts our state of hypnosis by reintroducing us to authenticity, integrity, and intimacy, and so gradually assists in toppling the false gods we worship.

Because it really works, others would want to bake it into a big pie so they could sell off the pieces for profit.

Others would want to form ‘Presence Process Groups’ so they could appear qualified and competent in something.

Individuals would attempt to use it as a means to project their unintegrated emotional condition upon others.

Hell, I may even get swept up in the tide of its impact and want to use it ‘to become somebody who is doing something special’! I am after all a Leo.

If this was truly to be an anti-virus program that worked – then wherever possible - the stickiness of unnecessary organizational mentality had to be circumvented. I had to protect it from this mentality with as much vigilance as I had to protect it from myself.

In 2005 I was fortunate to have The Presence Process published by Constance Kellough of Namaste Publishing Inc. She saw what this technology is and honored my approach to the work. She not only chose to be a vehicle to bring this work into the world – but she also entered the work experientially herself. Because of this, I knew the work was protected under her watch – and always shall be.

Upon publishing the book, Constance asked me if I – when the time came - would be prepared to personally seed this work in the world through traveling and delivering presentations.

I agreed – but not without careful inner scrutiny.

I realized that because the impact of The Presence Process is real – and because I was choosing to be the initial vehicle for delivering it into human awareness – people may make the common perceptual mistake of identifying with me, and not the work.

Most of us human beings, especially when we are emotionally undeveloped, are looking for ‘a guru’ - or at least someone to fix us - or at least someone to tell us what to do.

Only priests, politicians, pharmaceutical reps, advertising agencies, egomaniacal radio and television hosts, and cult leaders invite the opportunity at this point of our evolutionary experience to stand up in front of a crowd and behave as if they know what to do. And, only our hypnotic trance leads us into believing such individuals.

This unconsciously driven I-am-willing-to-following-anybody-but-me-mentality is counterproductive to any authentic intent for self-empowerment or self-facilitation.

The bottom line is that I had to be clear about what I was doing and what my part in this is. And, I had to keep reminding myself of this intent daily, especially when around those full of praise.

Most vigilance had to be applied whenever compliments are paid to me and not to the work.

People who paid me compliments obviously needed or wanted something. People who pay the work compliments are vulnerable to receiving something real from it. It is important to know the difference.

My role in this journey had to be purpose-driven and not career-orientated. In other words – I had a job to get done – as opposed to trying to turn this work into ‘my job’.

I had to recognize when to get out so that I did not become a caricature of myself flogging ‘the Presence Process catalogue of products’ on some public television station.

If I kept doing what I was doing just because I was successful at doing it – I would miss the point – and the boat! I would end up using The Presence Process to maintain a standard of living – which would dilute the integrity of the work.

And so, just over two years ago I left my comfortable home in South Africa, the wonderful company of my family, the cats I love, my facilitation practice, and headed for The United States and Canada. This kicked off both my presentation work and another level of one-on-one facilitation experiences.

Along the way I had to face myself over and over again. I had to make many seeming mistakes.

And, I consistently made many seeming mistakes. They were all valid. They served as my teachers - as my instructors in 'how to' and 'how not to'.

I often lost my way, forgot my task, and behaved unconsciously.

She set me free and patiently, through example, showed me how to set another free.

I know myself enough to know that keeping my intent clear, my feet on the ground, and my eyes on the prize would have been 1000 times more challenging had I not had her as my intimacy companion.

To say that the seeding experience of this work has been fun is an understatement. And, as fun as it has been, and still is as I now bring it to completion, it has also been extremely uncomfortable.

Through such experiences it became clear to me that although I was responsible for the way I portrayed myself and hence the work, I was definitely not alone in bringing it into accessibility.

In fact, it became obvious that every aspect of my journey had nothing to do with ‘teaching others’. That was just what it appeared to be on the surface. Rather, it was a rare and transient opportunity to become a full-time student in a very intense life-university.

Everyone proved to be my teacher!

It means arriving within the resonance of a journey in which all experience is responded to as valid.

It means entering 'the journey' as the only destination.

It means jumping off the edge of ‘the understood’ - beyond the mental body’s five-year-plan for success and accomplishment - and into the arms of the unfamiliar.

In other words – we reach completion when we trust that each moment is valid.

It is this simple and this powerful.

Yet, this cannot be understood – only experienced.
